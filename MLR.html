The price of a car, as set by the manufacturer, is based on the cost of production but can also be influenced by the desirability of features and quality of performance. Depending on the market the car is sold in, the manufacturer may charge more for a car model if it has multiple features which are in demand, thus increasing profits. Therefore different features will have different influences on the price of the car.

Backwards stepwise regression was implimented until all variables had a p value of less than 0.05. These are the features which most effect the price of the car. Using a train and test split on the data, the model was used to predict a price of a car based on these features and the results can be compared to the actual value. The results can be seen below. Dots that fall on the diagonal line indicate a perfect prediction by the model.

The R-squared score of these predictions is 0.91.
